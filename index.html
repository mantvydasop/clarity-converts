C.L.E.A.R. – What your homepage must do

Your homepage has one job. Make the value of your product clear.

C – Capture the right people.

Instantly signal who this is for and what problem you solve.

L – Land the core value.

Make your main promise so sharp your buyer can repeat it back.

E – Eliminate confusion.

Cut internal language and fluff that slow decisions down.

A – Arm buyers with proof.

Show specific, credible evidence that you can deliver the outcome.

R – Remove friction to action.

Make the next step obvious, low risk, and aligned with how they buy.

P.R.O.O.F. – How I turn complex products into conversion focused copy

You are not buying words. You are buying a system that makes your product easier to understand and easier to buy.

P – Probe the market.

Short, focused research to hear how buyers talk about the problem, what they compare you to, and what makes them switch. I use interviews when possible and when not I mine transcripts, reviews, support logs, founder notes, and competitor messaging.

R – Refine the message spine.

I map your messaging with Jobs To Be Done so we see moments, motivations, and switching triggers. Then I apply the Rule of One so the page rallies around one reader, one promise, one primary offer.

O – Orchestrate the page flow.

Using PCPO and CPTS I order the content so each section earns its place and naturally pulls the reader forward.

O – Optimize the words.

Every line has a job. To grab attention, build proof, remove a doubt, or drive action. No filler. No decoration. Just clean, persuasive language built for scanners.

F – Finalize, test, and hand off.

You get copy that is ready to ship. I deliver annotated Figma wireframes and a simple validation plan with clarity tests and lightweight analytics.
